## Description

Edric Ættfaðir Svartúlfa is recognized as the earliest known founder associated with the ancestral Svartúlfr family tradition.

The lineage originates during the Vendel Period in Scandinavia, prior to the Viking Age (which begins officially in 793 AD). This places the foundation of the Svartúlfr lineage at the dawn of the Viking era — the age of mythological kings, the sagas of Beowulf, and founders shrouded in legend.

This record serves as a historical dynastic origin point only and does not imply the existence of supernatural, mythological, or paranormal systems.

## Naming Authority

### Primary Canonical Name

**Edric Ættfaðir Svartúlfa**

Translation: "Edric, Father-Founder of the Svartúlfr Lineage"

### Accepted Historical Epithets

The following may appear in historical documents, family records, oral traditions, or cultural references:

| Epithets | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| Edric hinn Fyrsti Svartúlfr | "Edric the First Svartúlfr" |
| Edric Svartúlfr hinn Gamli | "Edric Svartúlfr the Old" |
| Edric Stofnandi Svartúlfaættar | "Edric, Founder of the Svartúlfr Line" |

These are recognized as historical titles and epithets referring to the same individual.

### Naming Convention Note

Per Norse tradition, Viking clans take their name from their founder — not the reverse. Edric does not "belong" to the clan of the Black Wolves. **He IS the first Black Wolf**, and all his descendants carry his name as a mark of lineage.
