## Scope

This record documents the historical commercial lineage of the Douglas Dynasty from its English origins through colonial expansion to California establishment.

This record operates entirely within Historical Canon. No changes are made to Active Canon genealogy, Family Authority records, Character Authority records, or the contemporary Los Angeles baseline.

---

## Historical Origin

### Douglas Dynasty — English Roots

The Douglas Dynasty originates in England. The family maintained English roots for multiple generations before migration to North America during the eighteenth century. The American branch descends from this English mercantile lineage.

*(Source: ADR-001 — Dynastic Origins)*

## Commercial Lineage

### Phase 1: Merchant House Douglas

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Foundation Date** | 1666 |
| **Classification** | Historical Canon |
| **Origin** | England |
| **Type** | Merchant House |
| **Founder** | Lord Cornelius Vance Douglas |

The earliest documented commercial organization associated with the Douglas family is a Merchant House established in England in 1666 by **Lord Cornelius Vance Douglas**. This enterprise represents the beginning of the family's commercial legacy.

**Founder Role:** Lord Cornelius Vance Douglas served as Governatore (Governor) of the California colony, establishing both the territorial and commercial foundations of the Douglas Dynasty in North America.

**Activities:**
- Maritime trade
- Merchant logistics
- Colonial supply operations
- Commercial investment

This organization predates the modern Douglas Commercial Coalition by several centuries.

### Phase 2: Douglas Colonial Trading Company

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Period** | 1700s |
| **Classification** | Historical Canon |
| **Origin** | Colonial North America |
| **Type** | Trading Company |

During the colonial era, the original Merchant House expanded into a larger trading organization commonly referred to as the Douglas Colonial Trading Company. The company became the primary vehicle through which the family established its commercial influence in North America.

The Colonial Trading Company represents the direct historical predecessor of later Douglas commercial enterprises.

### Phase 3: Douglas California Establishment

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Period** | Late 1500s–1700s |
| **Classification** | Historical Canon |
| **Nature** | Colonial governorship and territorial establishment |
| **Governor** | Lord Cornelius Vance Douglas |

Lord Cornelius Vance Douglas established the Douglas colonial presence in California as **Governatore (Governor) of the colony**. He is recognized as both the founder of the commercial enterprise (1666 Merchant House) and the territorial governor who established the family's California holdings.

This record represents colonial governance and dynastic territorial establishment.

## Governance Tradition

A recurring pattern throughout Douglas family history is the assumption of administrative, mercantile, and governing responsibilities. Historical records, family traditions, and alternate historical settings consistently associate senior Douglas figures with roles involving:

- Governance
- Colonial administration
- Commercial oversight
- Territorial stewardship
- Political leadership

This pattern is considered a recurring dynastic characteristic rather than evidence for any specific individual.

## Magnus Douglas Clarification

**Magnus Douglas is NOT the founder of the original Douglas commercial enterprise.**

Magnus Douglas is specifically associated with the Sci-Fi timeline and the future-era Douglas Commercial Coalition. The historical commercial lineage predates Magnus Douglas by centuries.

This clarification prevents cross-timeline contamination between Historical Canon and deferred/archived timeline content.
