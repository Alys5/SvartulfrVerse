## Overview

The UCLA Campus is the primary university setting for all student-related SvartúlfrVerse storylines. Located in the Westwood neighborhood of Los Angeles, it serves as the academic, social, and political hub for the younger generation of the Douglas-Bloodmoon family and their associated characters.

Where the Douglas Estate represents family legacy and control, UCLA Campus represents the younger generation's space — where they study, socialize, form identities independent of the family, and build relationships that exist outside Erik's surveillance.

## Key Campus Areas

### Royce Hall

UCLA's most recognizable building. Twin-towered Italian Romanesque Revival architecture serves as the university's visual symbol. Houses Royce Hall Auditorium (~1,800 seats) for major lectures, concerts, and convocations.

**Narrative function:** Major institutional events, high-profile lectures, public-facing ceremonies, scenes requiring academic grandeur.

### Powell Library

UCLA's main undergraduate library. Northern Italian Romanesque Revival building adjacent to Royce Hall. Primary study space for undergraduates, common meeting point, quiet campus-adjacent conversation space.

**Narrative function:** Study scenes, chance encounters between classes, quiet conversations, academic atmosphere.

### Bruin Walk

The central pedestrian corridor running north–south through campus from Bruin Plaza toward the residence halls. Primary foot traffic artery and the site of student organization tabling, street performances, and daily campus life.

**Narrative function:** Casual encounters, public confrontations, ambient campus atmosphere, announcement delivery.

### Bruin Plaza

Open concrete plaza at the southern terminus of Bruin Walk, directly in front of Ackerman Union. Primary outdoor gathering space for student events, rallies, fairs, and demonstrations.

**Narrative function:** Political organizing, public demonstrations, large-scale social gatherings, high-visibility scenes.

### Ackerman Union

The primary student union building at the base of Bruin Walk. Houses dining options, meeting rooms, student organization offices, bookstore, and primary indoor social space.

**Narrative function:** Meals, student organization operations, casual social interaction, multi-level layout for varied space types.

### Kerckhoff Hall

Administrative building housing student government offices, media organizations (Daily Bruin), and additional student organization spaces. Institutional atmosphere distinct from the social energy elsewhere on campus.

**Narrative function:** Student government storylines, journalism/media plots, organizational leadership scenes.

### Janss Steps

Iconic stepped walkway connecting upper campus (Royce Hall, Powell Library) to lower campus (Ackerman Union, Bruin Plaza). Natural gathering point and campus landmark.

**Narrative function:** Transitional scenes, planned meetings at a landmark, moments of decision.

### Pauley Pavilion

UCLA's primary basketball and indoor athletics venue. Seats approximately 13,800. Intense crowd atmosphere for rivalry games and championship events.

**Narrative function:** Major athletic events, high-stakes competitions, pep rallies, large indoor gatherings.

### Sunset Recreation Center

UCLA's primary recreation and fitness facility. Gymnasiums, pools, weight rooms, courts, and group fitness programming. Located near residence halls.

**Narrative function:** Casual athletics, training, early-morning or late-night character moments, informal social interaction.

## Social Infrastructure

### Greek Life

UCLA hosts approximately 60 Greek organizations with ~4,100 participating students. The Kappa Sigma Alpha (KSA) fraternity holds particular significance in the SvartúlfrVerse narrative as the multi-generational Douglas-Bloodmoon family tradition.

For full details, see [I_UCLA_GreekLife.md](../institutions/I_UCLA_GreekLife.md) and [I_KappaSigmaAlpha.md](../institutions/I_KappaSigmaAlpha.md).

### Student Organizations

Over 1,000 registered student organizations spanning academic, cultural, political, recreational, and professional categories. The Undergraduate Students Association Council (USAC) serves as student government.

For full details, see [I_UCLA.md](../institutions/I_UCLA.md) and [I_UCLA_StudentOrganizations.md](../institutions/I_UCLA_StudentOrganizations.md).

### Campus Housing

UCLA's residential system includes residence halls (primarily for freshmen) and university apartments (upperclassmen). Greek Life houses cluster in the residential blocks west of Gayley Ave. Housing cost and availability create ongoing student stress.

## Narrative Function

### Academic Hub

UCLA is the primary academic setting for all student characters. Classes, study sessions, professor interactions, academic pressure, and intellectual development all center on campus life.

### Social Mobility

The campus provides a narrative space where characters of different backgrounds interact on relatively equal footing. The Douglas name carries weight, but campus anonymity also allows characters to define themselves beyond family identity.

### Emerging Relationships

Romantic relationships, friendships, rivalries, and alliances form most naturally in the campus environment. The density of student life — classes, organizations, housing, dining — creates constant proximity and encounter opportunities.

### Student Politics

USAC, Greek councils, and student organization leadership provide political narrative infrastructure. Elections, budget debates, and institutional power struggles mirror larger family and corporate politics at a personal scale.

### Campus Autonomy vs. Family Control

The campus represents the tension between the younger generation's desire for independence and the family's protective control. Erik's surveillance reaches campus (biometric watches, DCC Security presence), but the campus also provides spaces where family control is reduced. This tension drives significant narrative conflict.
