## Overview

Douglas Customs is Logan Douglas's automotive workshop, located in the Arts District of Downtown Los Angeles only a few blocks from Verve Lounge. The shop specializes in vehicle restoration, customization, fabrication, and performance work.

Where The Verve expresses Logan's creative and social side, Douglas Customs expresses his hands-on technical identity. The workshop is Logan's primary workplace — the place he retreats to when the family's corporate world becomes suffocating.

## Architecture and Design

Like The Verve, Douglas Customs occupies a converted industrial property — part of the Arts District's warehouse heritage. However, where The Verve has been renovated for aesthetic appeal, the workshop prioritizes function:

- **Bay doors** — large rolling doors for vehicle access
- **High ceilings** — clearance for lifts and large vehicles
- **Concrete floors** — oil-stained, functional, easy to clean
- **Industrial lighting** — high-output work lighting over each bay
- **Steel and raw materials** — exposed structure, minimal decoration
- **Tool organization** — Logan's personal system, visible on walls and benches

The workshop's aesthetic is honest. There is nothing decorative — every surface, every tool, every material serves a function. This contrasts sharply with the Douglas Estate's polished formality and reflects Logan's rejection of corporate aesthetics.

## Key Areas

### Restoration Bays

Primary work areas for vehicle restoration projects. Two full-size bays equipped with vehicle lifts, pneumatic tools, and diagnostic equipment. Most of Logan's restoration work happens here — returning classic vehicles to original condition or better.

**Narrative function:** Logan at work, character bonding over mechanical projects, vehicle backstory revelation, hands-on collaboration scenes.

### Fabrication Area

A dedicated space for custom metalwork and fabrication. Welding equipment, metal shears, bending brakes, and raw materials. Logan fabricates custom parts when off-the-shelf solutions don't exist — a common occurrence with vintage and rare vehicles.

**Narrative function:** Logan's creative-technical problem-solving, custom build projects, Logan's craftsmanship on display.

### Motorcycle Workstations

Specialized work areas for motorcycle maintenance and customization. Different from the main bays — lower work tables, motorcycle-specific lifts, precision tooling. The Harley-Davidson at The Verve is maintained here, along with Logan's personal motorcycles.

**Narrative function:** Mculture scenes, motorcycle riding preparation, Logan's personal collection, two-wheeled escape sequences.

### Tool Room

A climate-controlled room housing Logan's precision tool collection — diagnostic computers, calibration equipment, specialty tools, and measuring instruments. This room represents Logan's investment in his craft: tools are expensive, well-maintained, and organized with precision.

**Narrative function:** Logan's meticulous nature, tool knowledge scenes, teaching moments (Logan showing someone how tools work).

### Office Mezzanine

A raised office space overlooking the workshop floor. Minimal desk, computer, parts catalogues, project files. Logan manages business operations from here — client communication, parts ordering, project scheduling. The mezzanine also serves as an observation point: Logan can see the entire workshop floor from above.

**Narrative function:** Business discussions, Logan's dual role (craftsman and small business owner), project planning.

### Vehicle Storage

A secure area for client vehicles awaiting work or pickup. Also stores Logan's personal vehicles and project vehicles in various states of completion. The storage area tells the story of Logan's backlog and priorities.

**Narrative function:** Vehicle reveals, project progress tracking, Logan's workload pressure.

## Logan's Daily Rhythm

Logan's typical pattern involves splitting time between Douglas Customs and The Verve:

- **Morning/Afternoon:** Workshop — focused mechanical work, client projects
- **Evening:** The Verve — managing the venue, social time, live performance oversight
- **Late Night:** The Verve rooftop or penthouse — decompression, private time, motorcycle maintenance

This rhythm keeps Logan grounded in physical work while maintaining his social space. The workshop is where he goes when he needs to think with his hands.

## Narrative Function

### Logan's Primary Workplace

Douglas Customs is Logan's professional identity. He is not Douglas family management — he crafts things with his hands. This distinction is central to Logan's character and drives his independence from Erik's corporate world.

### Character Bonding

The workshop provides a natural setting for character bonding through shared activity. Working on a vehicle together — Logan and a family member, Logan and a friend, Logan and a romantic interest — creates organic dialogue opportunities. Physical work lowers conversational barriers.

### Mechanical Projects

Restoration and customization projects structure narrative time. A multi-chapter vehicle restoration mirrors character development: dismantling (exposing truth), repair (healing), rebuilding (growth), finishing (transformation). The workshop makes this metaphor physical and visible.

### Motorcycle Culture

The motorcycle workstations and Mculture elements connect to broader LA motorcycle culture — cruising, club rides, vintage bike culture, the freedom of two wheels. This extends the workshop's narrative range beyond the building itself.

### Working-Class Contrast

Douglas Customs grounds the Douglas family narrative in something tangible. The family's billions in Beverly Hills feel abstract; a hand-built engine or a vintage restoration makes wealth feel earned. Logan's workshop legitimizes the family through craft rather than capital.
