## Overview

Seven Hills Estate is the ancestral seat of the Douglas family in California — a property held since the English colonial era, originally established as the regional headquarters of the Douglas Commercial Company (DCC). The estate occupies a parcel of grazing land and attached woodland, with a well-maintained and restored 1700s Georgian-style villa at its center.

For SvartúlfrVerse, Seven Hills represents the **deepest layer of Douglas family identity** — older than Beverly Hills, older than DCC's modern corporate form, older than the family's American identity. It is where the Douglas commercial power was first anchored in North American soil, and it remains a living connection to the Merchant House lineage founded in 1666.

## Historical Foundation

### The Merchant-Governor Context

The estate's origin lies in the complex commercial politics of 18th-century colonial California. Under Spanish rule, mercantile laws were strict — but English ships of the Douglas Commercial Company operated under special licenses and secret trade agreements (Asiento-type arrangements) that permitted British merchants to supply Spanish missions with manufactured goods in exchange for hides, tallow, and timber.

The Lord Douglas in question was not an official colonial governor (impossible under Spanish sovereignty) but a **Port Governor** or **Trade Commissioner** operating within a commercial grey zone. Leveraging his company's influence, he obtained from the Viceroy of New Spain the right to establish a **logistics outpost** — the foundation of what became Seven Hills Estate.

### The Trading Post (c. 1740 – 1820)

From its founding through the early 19th century, Seven Hills functioned as the **quartermaster headquarters** of the Douglas Commercial Company's colonial operations:

- **Warehousing** — storage for goods arriving by sea and destined for inland distribution
- **Diplomatic interface** — neutral ground for negotiations between DCC merchants and Spanish colonial authorities
- **Logistics coordination** — management of supply flows between ships docking along the coast (modern Santa Monica area) and inland routes through the wooded hinterland
- **Timber management** — the attached woodland served as the DCTC's natural resource reserve for ship maintenance and construction

The British Georgian architecture was a deliberate statement of authority and prestige — a visual declaration of DCC's commercial power in territory nominally controlled by Spain.

### Transition to Family Estate (1820 – Present)

As the colonial era ended and California passed through Mexican and then American sovereignty, Seven Hills transitioned from commercial headquarters to **private family property**. The Douglas family maintained it through every political change, preserving the original structures and woodland.

Today, the estate serves as the family's "country house" — a secondary property now that the primary residence is the Douglas Estate in Beverly Hills. The woodland preserves its 17th-century configuration, adapted for high-intensity training at altitude and isolation.

## Key Areas

### The Villa (c. 1740)

The central residence. British Georgian architecture — symmetrical facade, multi-pane sash windows, hipped roof, brick construction. Restored multiple times but maintaining original proportions and materials. The villa contains:

- **Great Hall** — original DCC trading floor, now formal reception space
- **Governor's Study** — the Merchant-Governor's original office, preserved with period furnishings
- **Family Quarters** — private living spaces updated through the centuries while maintaining Georgian character
- **Wine Cellar** — original foundation-level storage, now housing family wine collection
- **Library** — houses DCC commercial records, shipping logs, and family documents dating to the 1700s

**Narrative function:** Family history scenes, inheritance discussions, moments of connection to the Douglas commercial legacy, quiet reflection on generational continuity.

### The Woodland Reserve

The attached woodland preserves its original 17th-century configuration. Once the DCTC's timber reserve for ship maintenance, the forest now serves as:

- **Private training ground** — high-intensity physical training in isolation, used by Malachia for fight preparation
- **Hunting preserve** — traditional family hunting grounds
- **Meditation and recovery space** — the forest's silence and natural setting provide psychological decompression
- **Trail network** — maintained paths through the woodland for running, cycling, and tactical movement drills

**Narrative function:** Malachia training scenes, family outdoor gatherings, solitary character moments, nature-as-character themes.

### The Training Camp

Modern training facilities integrated into the estate's natural landscape. Designed to support Malachia's dual identity as professional athlete and academic:

- **Outdoor boxing ring** — open-air training space with woodland backdrop
- **Strength and conditioning area** — equipment for high-intensity functional training
- **Biomechanics observation deck** — elevated platform for studying movement patterns (connecting to Malachia's PhD research)
- **Recovery facilities** — cold plunge, sauna, stretching areas

**Narrative function:** Malachia's training discipline, the intersection of athletic and academic identity, family members visiting during training camps.

### The Gardens and Grounds

Landscaped grounds surrounding the villa, blending formal English garden design with the natural California landscape:

- **Formal gardens** — manicured hedges, rose gardens, reflecting pools
- **Grazing land** — original pastureland, maintained as open space
- **Entry drive** — a long, tree-lined approach road that creates a sense of arrival and separation from the outside world

**Narrative function:** Arrival scenes, garden conversations, outdoor family events, visual atmosphere.

```

    ↓
```

## Malachia's Connection

Seven Hills Estate is particularly significant for Malachia Douglas-Bloodmoon:

- **Training base** — Malachia uses the estate's woodland and training camp for fight preparation, combining the isolation of the forest with modern training facilities
- **Academic inspiration** — the estate's history of disciplined commercial management mirrors his own approach to boxing: methodical, strategic, precise
- **Generational weight** — training in a structure that has "managed power" for centuries reinforces Malachia's sense of dynastic responsibility
- **The forest as laboratory** — the woodland's natural terrain provides real-world biomechanics testing grounds that complement his PhD research

Malachia's relationship with Seven Hills embodies his dual identity: the athlete who trains in the forest, the scholar who studies in the library, the heir who carries forward a legacy that began with a trading post in 1740.

## Current Use

### Primary Function: "Country House"

Now that the family's primary residence is the Douglas Estate in Beverly Hills, Seven Hills serves as:

- **Weekend retreat** — family members visit for privacy and decompression
- **Training camp** — Malachia's primary fight preparation location
- **Family gatherings** — holidays, celebrations, and important family meetings
- **Historical preservation** — the family maintains the estate as a living museum of their own history

### Security

Seven Hills operates under lighter security than the Beverly Hills compound — the estate's isolation and private ownership provide natural protection. DCC Security maintains a perimeter presence but the atmosphere is significantly less surveilled than the main estate.
