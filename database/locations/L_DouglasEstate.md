## Overview

The Douglas Estate is the primary residence and symbolic seat of the Douglas family in Los Angeles. The property occupies several acres in North Beverly Hills and represents generations of accumulated family wealth, influence, and legacy.

For SvartúlfrVerse, the Estate is the gravitational center of the Douglas family narrative — the place where family power is most visible, where conflicts are most intense, and where the tension between old-world tradition and modern corporate life plays out in physical space.

## Key Areas

### Main Mansion

The central residence. Traditional Beverly Hills architecture blended with modern security infrastructure. Multiple wings accommodate both private family spaces and formal entertaining areas. The mansion is designed for simultaneous privacy (family can retreat to private quarters) and impressive public presentation (formal entertaining spaces visible to guests).

**Narrative function:** Family dinners, formal gatherings, private family conflicts, Erik's domain.

### Formal Dining Hall

A large, traditional dining space used for the family's mandatory Sunday lunches — which function as informal family government meetings. The long mahogany table seats the entire family and occasional guests. Erik sits at the head. Wulfnic sits at the far end when present.

**Narrative function:** Family government, Sunday lunch scenes, formal announcements, inheritance discussions, family conflicts.

### Family Library

A traditional library housing family records, historical documents, and the accumulated intellectual property of multiple Douglas generations. Wulfnic is most frequently found here, reading or sharing family history with younger family members.

**Narrative function:** Character exposition, Wulfnic-centered scenes, family history revelations, quiet private conversations.

### Private Gardens

Landscaped grounds surrounding the mansion. Provide outdoor space for private conversation, family gatherings, and the physical separation between the mansion's public and private areas.

**Narrative function:** Private conversations, outdoor family events, garden parties, romantic scenes.

### Guest Wing

A separate wing of the mansion designated for visiting family members and guests. When family members visit from out of town, this is their quarters. Also used for important business guests who require overnight accommodation.

**Narrative function:** Guest character introductions, extended visits, outside perspective on family dynamics.

### Security Gatehouse

The primary entry point controlled by DCC Security — Black Wolf Division. All visitors pass through biometric screening and identity verification. The gatehouse represents the boundary between the family's private world and the outside.

**Narrative function:** Security scenes, arrival/departure scenes, tension between family protection and outside access.

### Underground Garage

A subterranean garage housing the family's vehicle collection — armored SUVs, vintage muscle cars, luxury vehicles, and {{user}}'s bright yellow convertible. The garage is also a known escape route used by Jasper for unauthorized exits.

**Narrative function:** Vehicle scenes, Jasper's sneak-outs, family wealth display, escape sequences.

### Private Event Spaces

Multiple rooms within the compound designed for entertaining — ballrooms, terraces, and reception areas used for corporate galas, family celebrations, and high-society gatherings. The Douglas family's social obligations require these spaces.

**Narrative function:** Gala events, corporate entertaining, society gatherings, major family celebrations.

## Occupant Zones

### Erik's Domain ("The Throne Room")

Erik's private office and command center within the mansion. Combines corporate command capability (screens showing security feeds, financial data, DCC operations) with personal space. Erik conducts family governance from this room as much as corporate governance.

**Narrative function:** Erik's private moments, family confrontations, security crisis management, Erik-Logan brotherly interactions.

### Malachia's East Wing

Malachia's quarters within the compound, located in the east wing. Reflects his disciplined personality — functional, organized, security-conscious. Also serves as his operational base when coordinating family protection.

**Narrative function:** Malachia-centered scenes, security planning, quiet elder-brother moments.

### Wulfnic's Private Library and Quarters

Wulfnic maintains his own dedicated space within the compound — a private library and adjoining living quarters. This space reflects Bloodmoon heritage more than Douglas corporate culture: Icelandic texts, traditional furnishings, cultural artifacts.

**Narrative function:** Wulfnic-centered scenes, family history exposition, quiet wisdom moments, Bloodmoon cultural scenes.

### Jasper's Studio

Jasper maintains a private studio space within the compound — equipped with DJ equipment, computers, and audio gear. The family tolerates this space despite its contrast with the mansion's formal aesthetic. It represents Jasper's double life existing within the family fortress.

**Narrative function:** Jasper's music scenes, hacking operations, private twin moments, rebellion within the compound.

### Alyssa's Solarium

A bright, warm room filled with natural light — Alyssa's favorite space within the compound. Plants, sunlit windows, comfortable seating. The solarium represents Alyssa's role as the family's emotional warmth and her connection to Nixara's memory.

**Narrative function:** Alyssa-centered scenes, Nixara memory moments, emotional family scenes, twin bonding.

### Noah's Kitchen Access

Noah has particular access to and interest in the mansion's kitchen facilities. His late-night baking tradition — making pastries and desserts for family members — is centered here.

**Narrative function:** Noah's nurturing side, quiet family moments, food as emotional expression.

## Narrative Function

### Family Gatherings

The Estate is the mandatory gathering point for all major family events. Sunday lunches, holiday celebrations, graduation parties, and crisis meetings all occur here. The family's physical proximity in the Estate's dining room and common spaces creates unavoidable interaction.

### Family Conflicts

The Estate's enclosed nature intensifies family conflicts. Characters cannot easily leave — the walls, gates, and security perimeter create a pressure cooker. Arguments in the Estate have nowhere to dissipate.

### Inheritance and Legacy

The Estate embodies generational wealth and the question of inheritance. Its maintenance, expansion, and future ownership are recurring themes. Who will inherit? How will the family legacy continue? The Estate makes these questions tangible.

### Introduction Point

New characters are typically introduced to the family at the Estate. Whether through formal invitation (dinner party, gala) or forced entry (security breach, unexpected arrival), the Estate is the threshold between family insiders and outsiders.

### Major Canon Events

The Estate is the setting for the family's most significant canon events: Erik's announcements, family votes on important matters, crisis responses, and moments of collective family decision-making.
