## Early Life

Wulfnic Bloodmoon was born in 1948 in the United States to Icelandic immigrant parents who arrived during the 1930s-1940s migration period. As the first American-born Bloodmoon, he grew up straddling two worlds: the Old World traditions of his parents' homeland and the emerging American postwar prosperity.

---

## Family Heritage

The Bloodmoon family carries the Svartúlfr heritage — the tradition of the Black Wolves from Iceland. Wulfnic was raised with the oral histories, legends, and cultural practices of his ancestors. He became the designated custodian of this heritage, responsible for preserving and passing down the family's cultural memory.

---

## Adult Life

Wulfnic established himself as a figure of quiet authority within the Bloodmoon family. Unlike the Douglas dynasty's corporate power, Wulfnic's influence came from his role as keeper of tradition, family historian, and cultural authority.

---

## Relationship with Nixara

Wulfnic's daughter Nixara Bloodmoon (born 1975) was his primary focus after his wife's death. He raised her with deep connection to both her Icelandic heritage and her American identity. Her marriage to Erik Douglas in the 1990s created the Douglas-Bloodmoon dynastic union.

---

## Loss and Legacy

Nixara's death during childbirth in 2005, delivering twins Jasper and Alyssa, was a devastating blow to Wulfnic. He became even more protective of his grandchildren, particularly the twins who carry the strongest visual resemblance to their mother.

---

## Current Status

As Bloodmoon Patriarch, Wulfnic serves as:
- Custodian of family memory
- Cultural authority for Bloodmoon traditions
- Bridge between the Douglas corporate power and Bloodmoon cultural heritage
- Grandfather and protector to the Douglas-Bloodmoon heirs
