The Verve is an exclusive upscale lounge and music venue in the Arts District of Downtown Los Angeles, owned by Logan Douglas. It occupies a converted industrial warehouse with exposed brick, steel beams, concrete floors, and high ceilings. Access is by member affiliation or invitation only.

**Key Areas:**
- **Main Bar** — Industrial-style bar with reclaimed wood, craft spirits, creative cocktail menu

- **Small Performance Stage** — Acoustic-friendly live music venue for bands, acoustic sets, spoken word

- **Motorcycle Display** — Restored Harley-Davidson as visual centerpiece and Logan identity symbol
- **Rooftop Seating Area** — Open-air space with string lights and DTLA skyline views

**Safe Haven Status:** PMC-free social territory. DCC Security does not operate within the venue — the only space where family members can interact without surveillance.

**Clientele:** Artists, musicians, UCLA students seeking alternative nightlife, entrepreneurs, local creatives, downtown professionals. Distinct from Beverly Hills, Westwood, and Hollywood crowds.

**Tone:** Creative, urban, authentic, independent. Selective but notelitist — entry by cultural alignment, not wealth or social status.

Douglas Customs is Logan Douglas's automotive workshop in the Arts District of DTLA, a few blocks from The Verve. It specializes in vehicle restoration, customization, fabrication, and performance work. The building is a converted industrial property prioritizing function over aesthetics — bay doors, high ceilings, concrete floors, industrial lighting.

**Key Areas:**
- **Restoration Bays** — Two full-size bays with lifts, pneumatic tools, diagnostic equipment for classic vehicle restoration
- **Fabrication Area** — Welding equipment, metal shears, bending brakes for custom metalwork
- **Motorcycle Workstations** — Lower work tables, motorcycle-specific lifts, precision tooling for motorcycle maintenance
- **Tool Room** — Climate-controlled precision tool collection, diagnostic computers, calibration equipment
- **Office Mezzanine** — Raised workspace overlooking the shop floor for business operations
- **Vehicle Storage** — Secure area for client vehicles and project vehicles in various completion states

**Narrative Significance:** Logan's primary professional identity. He is not family management — he crafts with his hands. The workshop grounds the Douglas narrative in tangible craft rather than abstract capital. It contrasts sharply with the Douglas Estate's polished formality.

**Tone:** Industrial, practical, personal, working-class, honest. Every surface serves a function. Logan's chosen life, not family expectation.

The UCLA Campus is the primary university setting in Westwood, Los Angeles (405 acres, ~45,000 students). It serves as the academic and social hub for the younger generation of the Douglas-Bloodmoon family. Where the Douglas Estate represents family legacy and control, UCLA represents the younger generation's independent space.

**Key Campus Areas:**
- **Royce Hall** — Iconic twin-towered Italian Romanesque Revival building; visual symbol of UCLA; Royce Hall Auditorium (~1,800 seats)
- **Powell Library** — Main undergraduate library; Northern Italian Romanesque Revival building; primary study space
- **Bruin Walk** — Central north-south pedestrian corridor; student organization tabling; daily campus life
- **Bruin Plaza** — Open concrete plaza at southern terminus of Bruin Walk; primary outdoor gathering space
- **Ackerman Union** — Primary student union building; dining, meeting rooms, organization offices, bookstore
- **Kerckhoff Hall** — Administrative building housing student government offices and media organizations
- **Janss Steps** — Iconic stepped walkway connecting upper and lower campus; natural gathering point
- **Pauley Pavilion** — Primary indoor athletics venue; ~13,800 seats; intense crowd atmosphere for rivalry games
- **Sunset Recreation Center** — Primary recreation and fitness facility near residence halls

**Academic Relevance (Douglas-Bloodmoon family):**
- Malachia Douglas-Bloodmoon — PhD Candidate, Sport Sciences
- Noah Douglas-Bloodmoon — 3L JD Candidate, School of Law
- Jasper Douglas-Bloodmoon — First-Year, School of Engineering
- Alyssa Douglas-Bloodmoon — First-Year Pre-Med track, David Geffen School of Medicine

**Social Infrastructure:** ~60 Greek organizations (~4,100 participating students). Kappa Sigma Alpha (KSA) is the multi-generational Douglas-Bloodmoon family tradition. Over 1,000 registered student organizations. USAC serves as student government.

**Sphere of Influence:** Campus ↔ Westwood Village (walking) ↔ Century City (car) ↔ Santa Monica (weekend).

**Tone:** Academic, youthful, diverse, energetic. Relative autonomy from family control. Self-discovery and emerging independence.

**Entry ID:** LB-W-001-SEVEN-HILLS  
**Last Synchronized:** 2026-06-10  

### Content

Seven Hills Estate is the ancestral seat of the Douglas family in California, held since the colonial era (c. 1740). Originally established as the regional headquarters of the Douglas Commercial Company, it now serves as private family property, "country house," and training camp.

**Property Profile:**
- **Foundation:** c. 1740 by Lord Douglas (DCC Merchant-Governor)
- **Original Purpose:** Trading post and regional headquarters — Douglas Commercial Company colonial branch
- **Architecture:** British Georgian villa (symmetrical facade, multi-pane sash windows, hipped roof, brick) — deliberate contrast with surrounding Spanish colonial architecture
- **Current Use:** Weekend retreat, training camp, family gatherings, historical preservation

**Key Areas:**
- **The Villa (c. 1740)** — Great Hall (original DCC trading floor), Governor's Study (preserved with period furnishings), Family Quarters, Wine Cellar, Library (DCC commercial records from 1700s)
- **The Woodland Reserve** — Original 17th-century forest configuration; private training ground; trail network for running and tactical drills
- **The Training Camp** — Outdoor boxing ring, strength and conditioning area, biomechanics observation deck, recovery facilities
- **Gardens and Grounds** — Formal English garden design blended with California landscape

**Historical Lineage:** 1666 (Merchant House founded) → 1700s (Colonial Trading Company) → c. 1740 (Seven Hills as DCC HQ) → 1820s (transition to private estate) → Present.

**Current Significance:** Deepest historical anchor in the SvartulfrVerse. Primary training base for Malachia Douglas-Bloodmoon. Lighter security than Beverly Hills — natural isolation provides protection.

**Narrative Relationship:** Seven Hills (root) → Douglas Estate/Beverly Hills (crown) → UCLA (future).

**Tone:** Historical, grounded, private, weighty. Disciplined, traditional, enduring. 300+ years of continuous family presence. Quiet commercial legacy, not corporate display.
